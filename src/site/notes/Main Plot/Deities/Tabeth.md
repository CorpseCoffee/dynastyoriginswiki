⊱Description:
Tabeth presents herself as a tall woman with bright blue eyes and short hair. 
She’s more humanoid when she is visualized by a mortal but that isn’t technically what she is. Her true appearance is more of an arrangement with no true form other than the one she gives herself. 

⊱Personality:
 Tabeth is very hot and cold with people. She views most people and things with a neutrality until given a reason to full lean one way. She tends to simply be sarcastically formal with everyone. 

⊱Holidays:
  Unnamed as of now-
For the whole third week of winter there is a holiday in which people will use magic to play tricks, pranks, or jokes on others. It’s also used as a way for newer magic users to try getting a better grasp of their magic. 
A week where magic semi runs wild.
It’s said that the more amusing the trick is to Tabeth the more of a blessing you will have from her in the upcoming year.


⊱Backstory:
 Tabeth was born a deity, created when the first flows of magic started to fall over the world. 
She is essentially magic personified, her very being is simply a construct of magic taking a full form. All magic is created and regulated by and through her.
It seeps from her body, much like an overflowing cup with holes cut into the bottom. It’s an endless recycling cycle. Once a being of magic dies, Tabeth holds onto it before passing it to the next magical creature. 
(Think of it as the water cycle)
She was very care free and sensitive when she first came into existence, happy to explore the extent of her power, taking the power of magic and forming it into specific types.
Over time she grew tired of this however, she could do any spell, any trick. She wanted to actually learn, to have to actually work, to understand. She grew envious of mortals and their ability to fail at things. The ability to grow as people. 
Tabeth eventually decided she didn’t want her domain, didn’t want this status. She wanted to pass it along but she knew she couldn’t just give her domains away. 
She has three children, each time hopping that maybe one of them would be able to carry the weight of her domain and let her be free of it. It didn’t work, she could not give away the very thing that made her a being.
She wasn’t a deity of magic, she was magic given deity firm. All her children were too human, to mortal, to unlike her to take her responsibility.
