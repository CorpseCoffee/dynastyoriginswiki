⟢God Sheet⟣
 ⊱Name: Vultheis the Bound Silence
 ⊱Symbols: A single cut eye with a reaching hand
 ⊱God of: Silence, and Sacrifice
 ⊱Description:
 Vultheis is often depicted as a towering, faceless being draped in black shrouds and chains, one hand always open in silent offering, the other clutching a curved ritual blade. No mouth, no eyes, only a void where its face should be. From its back trail hundreds of blackened cords or tendrils, like sculk vines, binding it to the world below.
It dwells in the Deep Vein, a mythic place beneath even the lowest crypts of [[Main Plot/Season 2/Caecus/Caecus\|Caecus]] where it listens to all secrets, screams, and prayers offered in silence.
⊱Personality:
 Vultheis does not speak. It does not answer. It receives. It is cold, patient, and unchanging. The faithful believe that Vultheis weighs the worth of a soul not by words or virtue, but by what one is willing to sacrifice in darkness and without witness.
 To the rare few who have claimed to sense it, they describe a presence that is enormous yet intimate as if being swallowed by something that once knew your name.
⊱Holidays:
The Stillmark: Once a year, Caecus observes a full day of utter silence. No words are spoken; even the streets fall empty. Offerings are made in blood, objects, or memory buried beneath stone or cast into deep sculk pits.


Vein's Rite: A sacred holiday reserved only for nobility or chosen heirs. It commemorates the ancient tradition of ritual combat between siblings or rivals, as a "gift" of sacrifice to Vultheis. It is not open to the public, but its outcome is publicly announced.


⊱Backstory:
 According to Caecian myth, Vultheis was not born of light or dark, it was the silence between the two. When the gods of creation shouted the world into being, Vultheis was the one who did not speak, and so what it knew remained hidden. Jealous of the noise and fury of other deities, Vultheis sank into the world’s roots and offered mortals something different. the power of secrecy and the strength of sacrifice without praise.
Its worship took root in Caecus, a kingdom beneath, where words are weapons and silence is survival. Kings and killers alike offer their guilt and glory to Vultheis, burying their sins in sculk and calling it devotion.
