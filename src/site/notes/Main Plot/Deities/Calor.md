⟢**Deity Sheet**⟣
⊱Name: Calor
⊱Symbols: Sun
⊱God of: Sun, Fire

⊱Deity Connections: 
[[Main Plot/Deities/Salem\|Salem]] - Close Friend
[[Main Plot/Deities/Pluto\|Pluto]] - Parent 
[[Main Plot/Deities/Tabeth\|Tabeth]] - close friend

⊱Character Connections: 
 Known by basically the entire kingdom of [[Solaris\|Solaris]]

⊱Description:
 A tall, humanoid, masculine figure with red skin and hair made of fire, which ends in a long braid. His clothing is mainly loose fabrics. Around his neck and ankles spark rings of fire, parts of his clothing glow with the heat of the sun. Instead of feet Calor has hooves. His eyes are bright white, glowing, and pupilless. Also very muscular. Can turn into fire and has an alternate animalistic form but almost never uses those.
⊱Personality:
 Calor is very outgoing, he loves being around people and is generally loud and extroverted and always open to meet new people. He is very busy though, since he has to keep the sun's temperature consistent in order to allow life on Earth to thrive. In his little free time he usually visits friends or goes drinking with other deities. If he were mortal he'd love bars and saloons.
⊱Holidays:
Eternal Bonfire: There is a big bonfire central to Solaris which the citizens of Solaris keep burning constantly to mimic and honor Calor's efforts of keeping the sun hot. Occasionally there are festivals and local events around the bonfire, it is also used for prayer. Any gifts to Calor are thrown into the flames, usually those gifts consist of fuel, but sometimes people throw in other things as well. The people hope that in keeping the bonfire burning they are supporting Calor's endeavor of keeping the sun burning
⊱Backstory:
Back when life on earth started to become slightly more advanced and needed consistent sunlight, the deities realized that keeping the sun burning brightly and consistently enough to foster life was a demanding job, and since none of them were willing to devote a large amount of time towards keeping the sun burning, Pluto created Calor to fulfill said job. Calor fuels the sun by shooting divine fire at it in just the right amounts to allow life on earth to thrive. Essentially it's his job to make sure temperatures don't get too hot or too cold.
